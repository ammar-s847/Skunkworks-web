Who are we?

The Skunkworks (Team 1246) is a Robotics Team that competes in the FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) Robotics Competition. The team is located in Scarborough, Ontario CA and is hosted by the Agincourt Collegiate Institute.

Subteams

Mechanical Engineering

The Mechanical Team creates the skeleton of our Robot! The team uses industry-standard machining tools such as a Mill, Lathe, Band-saw, and Drillpress in order to precisely fabricate individual components of the robot. Once components are fabricated, they are assembled together, into a structurally integral and reliable Mechanical system.

The Design Team specializes in all aspects of CAD (Computer Aided Design) and Technical documentation of components and systems. They use tools standard to any engineer such as Solidworks or Fusion 360 software to design, prototype, and set constraints for any components of the robot.

Design

Control Systems

By analogy, If the Mechanical Team creates the skeleton of the robot, the Controls Team creates the Muscles and Nerves. The team uses efficient and safe protocols in wiring an FRC standard Control System which involves a Microcontroller, Power Distribution Panel, Voltage Regulator, Pneumatics Control Module, ESCs (Electronic Speed Controllers), and multiple Electric Motors.

The Software team develops the Brain of the Robot! The team programs using WPILib (FRC Standard Control Library) in a Java configured environment and utilizes rudimentary concepts in Object Oriented Programming, Computer Vision, Sensor Input handling, UX/UI design (for drive station logistics screen), and Control System programming for FRC Robots.

Business and Media

The Business and Media team are involved in promoting the team and building beneficial connections with Sponsors.

The Drive and Scounting Team are the actual players in the FRC Competition events. The Drivers are in charge of controlling the robot on the floor of the competition. The Scouting team are involved with communicating with other teams in order to build strategies and gain insightful information about our allies and competitors.
